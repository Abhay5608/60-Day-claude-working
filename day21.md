## Key Findings

### Digital Footprint
The dashboard highlighted how online activities contribute to an overall digital presence and exposure level.

### Privacy Score
Several privacy gaps were identified through data-sharing patterns and account visibility settings.

### Exposure Heatmap
The heatmap visualized high-risk areas where personal information could be exposed.

### Company Exposure Ranking
Displayed organizations with the highest access to user data.

### Digital Twin Profile
Showed how collected information can be used to build a detailed behavioral profile.

### Risk Radar
Provided a visual representation of privacy and security risks across multiple dimensions.

### Valuable Data Assets
Identified the most sensitive and valuable categories of personal data.

### Privacy Improvement Simulator
Demonstrated how specific privacy actions could improve the overall privacy score.

---

## Learnings

- Digital privacy is measurable through multiple indicators.
- Online services collect and process significant amounts of user data.
- Small privacy improvements can significantly reduce exposure risk.
- Data visualization makes privacy risks easier to understand.
- Interactive dashboards are effective tools for privacy awareness.
